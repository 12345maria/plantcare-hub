    Organic feeding — timing & amounts

    All suggestions here assume organic-only gardening: compost, manures from trusted sources, plant/animal meals approved for organic use, and home-made ferments from kitchen waste. Dilute homemade liquids more than you think; stop if plants burn or smell sour.

    Active growth

    Top-dress compost or worm castings lightly every few weeks, or water with diluted compost tea (see below) every 7–14 days. For pots, use about 1–2 cm compost surface refresh or tea diluted until it looks like weak tea.

    Flowers & fruit

    Favor potassium from banana peel tea, wood ash (tiny pinch, pH-aware), or kelp meal tea. Ease off heavy nitrogen (fresh manure, strong nettle tea) so plants don’t stay all leaves.

    Seedlings

    Wait for 2–4 true leaves. Use only very weak compost tea or rice-water ferment at ¼ strength—roughly one part liquid to three parts water if color is dark.

    Liquid fertilizers from kitchen waste

    Cover jars loosely; use non-chlorinated water if possible. Strain before watering soil (not leaves if sticky).

    Compost tea

    Steep a double handful of finished compost in 4–5 L water 24–48 hours, stir occasionally. Strain; dilute until pale amber; use within a day.

    Banana peel tea (K)

    Soak chopped peels in water 3–7 days out of direct sun. Dilute 1:5 with water; good during flowering/fruiting.

    Eggshell extract (Ca)

    Wash, dry, crush shells; steep in water 24+ hours or add vinegar dropwise until they fizz (then dilute heavily). Use sparingly; not a fast fix.

    Coffee grounds brew (mild N, acidic)

    Use cooled plain coffee or steep spent grounds in water overnight. Dilute at least 1:3; acid-lovers only; skip if soil is already acidic.

    Vegetable cooking water

    Water from boiling unsalted veg (potato, carrot, greens); cool completely. Mild trace feed—don’t use if salted or oily.

    Weed / nettle tea (N-rich)

    Submerge young nettle or comfrey greens in water 2–3 weeks (smelly = normal). Dilute 1:10 before use; powerful—rotate with plain water.

    Potting mix lab

    Recipes by use case. Percentages are by volume.

    From seed → strong transplant

    1. Use seed mix: light, sterile, holds moisture without sogginess.
    2. Moisten mix before filling trays; sow at depth on packet.
    3. After true leaves appear, pot up or thin; introduce very dilute organic compost tea only if needed.
